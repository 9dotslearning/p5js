8. Transparency

Tinker

Click ▶Play. The drawing appears gray at the edges and darker at the center. This effect is created by 2 new commands:
◇ noFill(); makes the inside of ellipses transparent. ◇ stroke(0, 100, 0, 0.2); where a fourth argument makes the ellipse borders partially transparent, so you see the borders but also see through them. The amount of transparency in a color is called its alpha.

Try these challenges: In stroke, change the decimal until you find one you like. You can use any decimal between 0 and 1. 0 makes shapes completely transparent, 1 makes shapes completely opaque (not transparent at all). Replace the stroke and fill commands with the two new commands below. What happens?
fill(330, 100, 100, 0.1);
noStroke();

TBD
The command noFill(); makes fill completely transparent, so you can see ellipses behind other ellipses. In previous drawings, each new ellipse obscured previous ellipses.
There's also a way to change transparency a little at a time (instead of turning it on or off completely). Notice that the stroke command includes fourth argument: stroke(0, 0, 100, 0.2)
This argument is called alpha. Alpha controls the amount of transparency a little at a time. An alpha of 0 is completely transparent, and an alpha of 1 is not at all transparent. You can set alpha to any number between 0 and 1.
