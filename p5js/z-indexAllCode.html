In your own script at replit.com, investigate the 6 highlighted numbers. Change them one at a time and then click Run after each change. What changed in the drawing? What is the biggest number you can use in each position? The smallest?

function setup() {
  createCanvas(640, 480);
  background(110);

}


function draw() {
  ellipse(400, 150, 50);
}

Click for answers. But investigate first!

Answers

In createCanvas, 640 is the width of the drawing area and 480 is its height. The drawing area is called the canvas, and its size is measured in pixels. A pixel is the smallest dot on the screen that can show color and is usually about 1/100 inch wide. So our canvas is about 6.4 inches wide x 4.8 inches tall.

The background command paints the canvas with gray color 110. Numbers range from 0 (black) to 255 (white), with lighter grays as numbers increase.

The ellipse command draws a circle or an oval with:

  • horizontal position at 400 pixels across the screen (about 2/3 of our canvas width)
  • vertical position at 150 pixels down the screen (about 1/3 of our canvas height)
  • width of 50 pixels (about 1/2 inch).
